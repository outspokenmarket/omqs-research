What a determinant really means

A 2×2 matrix can be seen as a transformation of the plane. Its determinant tells you how much area is stretched or compressed, and whether the transformation flips orientation.

det(A) = a·d − b·c
det(A) = 2·3 − 1·1 = 5

For a 2×2 matrix, the determinant is the signed area of the parallelogram formed by the two column vectors.

The determinant is positive, so the transformation keeps orientation and scales area by 5.

Idea 1

It measures area scaling

Start from the unit square. After the matrix transformation, that square becomes a parallelogram. The absolute value of the determinant is the new area.

Idea 2

The sign matters

A positive determinant keeps orientation. A negative determinant flips it, like a mirror combined with stretching or compression.

Idea 3

Zero means collapse

If the determinant is zero, the parallelogram has no area. The two vectors are linearly dependent, so the plane collapses onto a line.
